Aratea text: De signis caelis (redaction)

Parchment — II + 88 fols. + I — 310 × 210 mm — Klosterneuburg (?) — s. XII4/4s. XIII1/4

Collation: Quire structure according to description in manuscripta.at: 8.IV64+V74+IV82+III88

Pendrawings in red and violet ink. On f. 70v three rota-diagrams on of the terrestria zones, one stating the circumference of earth, and another combining zodiacal signs, months, elements and their qualities, and the path of the sun. (image) Illustrations of the constellations on ff. 71r-83v; portraits of the planets on f. 84r; Zodiacal circle with full figures of Sol and Luna in the middle on f. 86r; medalion of Sol in quadriga on f. 86v and medalion of Luna in biga on f. 88r.

History: The manuscript cannot to be found in any of the monastery catalogues from the 12-14th c. A. Haidinger, in Sehnsucht nach der Antike, ed. Huber Wolfgang, pp. 66-67 however found palaeographical similarities between this manuscript and others written in Klosterneuburg, as for example MS 20. — Exlibris on f. 88r > Iste liber est sancte marie de Neuburga.
