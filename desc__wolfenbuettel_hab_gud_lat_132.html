Composed manuscript

Parchment — i (paper) + 108 + i (paper) fols. — 225 × 197 mm — Corvey Abbey — s. IX2De signis coeliDigital facsimile at Herzog August Bibliothek

Collation: 1-78, 84(-1), 92, 108, 114, 12-158, 166(-3) Quires open with a hair side, h, f-f, h-h. The first two quires much harder thick parchment.

f. 3 and 4 cut upper margin; outer margin of f. 8 – probably originally blank and cut for second use.Layout: Ruled in dry point on hair side; 24 lines per page.

Script: Caroline minuscule

Hands: Scribe 1 wrote ff.1r-48v. Scribe 2 wrote ff.49-61r Scribe 3 wrote ff.61v-65r Scribe 4 wrote ff.65r-83r and appears togeter with Hand 5 in the remaining f.os 83v-108r.

Blank spaces left probably for illustrations in the text of De ordine ac positione on ff. 54v-60v. On f. 108v there is a human head drawn in ink. Coloured and ornamented initials.

Binding: Modern binding with red leather.

History: According to Ganz (p. 158) the manuscript was copied for Corvey Abbey partially copied by a Corbie scribe. — According to Gustav Milchsack there is a note on the lower margin of f.11v: > Sebastianus dei cultor.
