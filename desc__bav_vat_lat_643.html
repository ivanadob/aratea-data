Parchment — — Melk (?) — s. XII

Collation: ff.1-8, 9-17, 24-32, 33-40, 41-46, 47-55, 56-63, 64-71, 71a-76, 77-80 (f. 81 skiped or missing), 82-89, 90-98 (stub between ff.90-91), 99-100.

History: Based on the script as well as on early added German glosses the manuscript was written in s. XII in South Germany or Austria. The textual similarities with Melk, Benediktinerstiftes, Cod. 412 suggest the Benedictine Abbey of Melk as place of production. — From the 18th c. was in the .

Illustrations of the constellations, diagram of the zodiac, the moon and the sun in chariots and a map of the celestial zones. The stars are marked in red according to the positions stated in the text. The illustrated disctinguish between brighter and fainter stars: the one referred to as > splendior are circles filled with a red crosses on a black background, while those labelled > obscura are filled in dull dark-red.
